In case of miscarriage, the female employee shall, on production of a
medical certificate stating this, will be entitled to leave with salary at
the rate of maternity benefit, for a period of six weeks immediately
following the day of her miscarriage.

A female employee suffering from illness arising out of pregnancy,
delivery, premature birth of child or miscarriage shall, on production
of a medical certificate, be entitled, in addition to the above
mentioned period of absence allowed to her, as the case may be, to
leave with salary at the rate of maternity benefit for a maximum
period of one month.
Note:
Paternity leave for male employees : All male employees who have
been associated with the company for a period exceeding 6 months shall
be eligible to claim a leave of 15 paid days within 1 month of the birth of
their child for a maximum of two times during the tenure of their service
in the company.
General
Guidelines
1.
Intervening declared holidays/weekends falling in between the leave
period availed will not be counted for the purpose of calculation of
leave (except maternity leave) availed by the employee.
2.
Leaves can be clubbed together by an Employee provided the criteria
for availing any particular leave are duly met.
3.
No leave as mentioned above can be claimed as a right by the
employee. Though the management encourages employees to avail
leave permissible to any employee, it shall be the discretion of the
management to disallow any application for leave if the business so
demands or in case of any exigency of work.
4.
Entitlement and provision of leave as mentioned above are subject to
modification at any time during the year, should the management
decide so. Any change in the provisions of leave shall be informed to
employees and that shall be binding on all the employees.
5.
Any wilful absence from the work will attract disciplinary action.
6.
Where interpretation/ clarification of any of these leave rules
mentioned above is required, it shall be referred to the Admin Head
and his/ her decision shall be final and binding.
7.
If the employee leaves the organization during the year and has
already consumed more CL/SL than the pro-rata leaves accrued, then
the same will be adjusted in his F&F settlement.
