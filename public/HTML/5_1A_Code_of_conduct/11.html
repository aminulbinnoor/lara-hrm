RAISING CONCERNS
XI. Raising Concerns and Reporting Misconduct
If you have genuine concerns regarding conduct- whether by another employee, a
supervisor, customer, consultant, agent, supplier, other third party or yourself -
that may violate the law, regulations or company policy, you will report the activity,
at the earliest practicable stage, by contacting any of the following:

Your immediate supervisor

Your local human resources representative
By reporting your suspicions, in good faith, you are ensuring that the DIPI is not
exposed to any unnecessary risks and that the DIPI's image is not tarnished.
It may sometimes be easier to ignore potential misconduct. However, your
commitment to the integrity of the DIPI means that any legal or ethical issues must
never be ignored. Malpractice within the DIPI is taken very seriously and you will
not be victimized or subjected to any detriment if you raise concerns in good faith.
