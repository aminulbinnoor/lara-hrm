III.
Privacy and Employee Confidentiality
The DIPI pledges to comply with its data protection obligations. Access to
personal data is strictly limited to authorized employees who have a business
reason for processing the data. Those with access to personal data must only
use it for the purpose for which it was collected and must adhere to high
standards of confidentiality. Personal data must be accurate and, where
necessary, kept up to date.
You give your consent to the DIPI processing your personal data whether
obtained directly from you or from another source where it relates to
employment matters for a number of purposes (e.g. payroll). Personal data will
be handled in accordance with applicable laws and regulations and internal
policies and procedures applicable to your region/country, and will not be held
by the DIPI for longer than is necessary.
You acknowledge that your personal data may be processed on the
DIPI’s
behalf in other countries. If your personal data are transferred or held
overseas, the DIPI will ensure your rights as an individual are protected.
Personal data held on DIPI property, such as computers or hand held devices,
are not private. However, the DIPI is committed to respecting and protecting
privacy but in certain situations, such as a reasonable suspicion of misconduct,
we may be left with no alternative but to monitor personal forms of
communication.
